Current focus

Cithambara Diya is currently focused on original artworks for offices and selected private interiors in Colombo, with future cultural expansion into literature.

Refundable deposit: LKR 60,000
Monthly fee: LKR 3,500 to LKR 10,000 depending on the artwork selected.

Sri Lanka has no shortage of artistic talent. What it lacks is enough space, attention and sustained opportunity for artists to remain visible and valued. Many gifted artists lose momentum, turn away from their craft, or remain unseen, not because of lack of ability, but because the culture around them does not support long-term appreciation.

Cithambara Diya exists to change that. Our purpose is to connect original Sri Lankan artworks with interiors that can host them meaningfully — offices, professional spaces and homes that can afford a deeper engagement with art. By doing so, we aim to create greater exposure for artists while encouraging a more refined and lasting relationship with beauty, thought and cultural expression.

Our model allows clients to make a refundable deposit of LKR 60,000 and rotate selected artworks on a monthly basis, with a monthly fee generally ranging from LKR 3,500 to LKR 10,000 depending on the artwork selected. Clients receive a curated portfolio of artworks and choose pieces that suit their space, mood and identity.

We are currently beginning in Colombo, with the intention of enrolling artists from across Sri Lanka and gradually expanding the reach of the platform. While our first focus is visual art, Cithambara Diya is guided by a broader cultural mission: to improve the quality of life in Sri Lanka by strengthening aesthetic sensibility, artistic respect and meaningful forms of engagement with culture.

We believe culture can be shaped from the top down as well as from the ground up. When companies, institutions and individuals choose to engage seriously with art, they help create a wider public environment where better taste, deeper thought and stronger creative confidence can grow.

Featured artwork

Cultural oil painting

Guttila & Musila by Alexie Adams

Guttila was a master musician serving the king of Benares, while Musila was a young and ambitious student eager to learn the art of playing the Veena. Initially, Guttila refused to teach Musila, but after Musila cared for Guttila’s blind parents, he agreed to train him. Musila became highly skilled and eventually challenged Guttila, claiming he was equal to his teacher. The king arranged a musical contest between them. With divine intervention, Guttila emerged victorious, proving that a student should always respect and honor their teacher. Musila, exposed for his arrogance and ingratitude, was rejected by the people. This tale serves as a timeless lesson on humility, respect, and the importance of acknowledging one's mentors

Artist spotlight

Alexie Kenneth Adams

Alexie Kenneth Adams is a fine artist with over 40 years of painting experience, whose work is rooted in classical traditions while also pushing their boundaries through original pieces and thoughtful reworkings of classical fine art. Over the years, he has developed a distinctive style shaped by technical exploration, cultural resonance and sustained artistic commitment. His works often draw on cultural themes and are created to evoke reflection, emotion and a renewed way of seeing. Through this practice, he continues to build a body of work that is both grounded in tradition and open to new expression.
